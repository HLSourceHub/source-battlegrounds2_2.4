Light Infantry

British Light Infantry supplemented regular heavy infantry and played the role of skirmishers against the Continental Army and state militias. Armed with a Brown Bess Carbine, the light infantry is speedier than heavy infantry, allowing them to attack with buckshot or roundball, and then fall back to renew their attack later in the battle. They are especially effective at harassing American troops, creating a diversion and softening the enemy while heavy infantry deals the final blow to the rebels.
